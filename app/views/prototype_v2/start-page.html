The NHS lung cancer risk check will ask you some questions about your medical history and lifestyle to work out your risk of getting lung cancer.

Lung cancer screening aims to find lung cancer early, sometimes before you have symptoms. Early diagnosis can make lung cancer more treatable and make treatment more successful.

You are eligible for this service if you are:

  • aged between 55 and 74
  • registered with a GP surgery
  • someone who smokes or used to smoke

What to expect

We will ask you questions about:

  • your height and weight
  • your ethnicity
  • the highest level of education you completed
  • if you have ever had a cancer diagnosis
  • if your parents, siblings, or children have ever had a lung cancer diagnosis
  • your smoking habits

After you submit your answers you will be told if you could be at a higher risk of developing lung cancer or not.

If your result suggests you could be at a high risk of developing lung cancer you will be offered a CT scan.
