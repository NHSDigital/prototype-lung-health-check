Higher risk

Information:

You could be at a higher risk of developing lung cancer

You will need to book an appointment for a CT scan.

A CT scan will take detailed pictures of the inside of your chest. The scan is used to detect any abnormalities in your lungs.

Lung cancer is found in about 1-2% of participants who are scanned. The aim is to find lung cancer early. Early diagnosis can make lung cancer more treatable and make treatment more successful.

When booking your CT scan appointment you will be asked to confirm if you:

  • have had a CT scan in the last 12 months
  • have had a chest infection requiring antibiotics in the last 6 weeks
  • require any assistance or adjustments. For example, if you use a wheelchair or walking frame

Benefits of stopping smoking

Stopping smoking is a great way to stay healthy, protect the health of those around you, and enjoy a better quality of life.

When you quit, you:

  • lower your risk of cancer, lung disease, heart disease, stroke and dementia
  • protect your loved ones and pets from secondhand smoke
  • feel better mentally in as little as 6 weeks
  • save money - around Â£38 a week for the average smoker
  • increase energy levels
  • look better and have healthier skin

It's never too late to stop

When you stop smoking, your body can repair itself sooner than you might think. It does not matter how old you are, or how long you have smoked.

Information:

When you stop smoking, in:

  • 2 days, all harmful carbon monoxide leaves your body
  • 2 to 12 weeks, your circulation improves
  • 3 to 9 months, your lung function and breathing improves
  • 1 year, your risk of a heart attack halves compared to a smoker

To help stop smoking:

  • talk to your GP or pharmacy about stop smoking aids, medication and support services
  • set small goals - you're 5 times more likely to quit for good if you stop smoking for 28 days
  • reflect on any past attempts to quit and try new methods
